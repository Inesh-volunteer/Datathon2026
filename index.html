The Problem

Why This Matters

Opening a restaurant is one of the riskiest business decisions a person can make. Without the right data, new owners are flying blind — picking locations based on gut feeling rather than hard evidence about population density, income levels, migration trends, and local demographics.

We built a model that scores every zip code in LA so first-time owners can make informed, data-backed decisions before signing a lease.

Our Approach

How We Built It

📥
01 — COLLECT
Data Ingestion
Loaded 5 CSVs covering consumer behavior (10K rows), migration patterns, zip-level demographics, property values, and address data across LA and OC.
↓
🧹
02 — CLEAN
Preprocessing
Filled missing values with medians and modes, dropped fully-null columns, and standardized column names so all 5 datasets could be merged on zip code.
↓
⚙️
03 — ENGINEER
Feature Creation
Derived new features not present in the raw data: Wealth Index, Population Density, Business/Residential Ratio, Higher Education Ratio, and Net Migration per zip.
↓
🏆
04 — SCORE
SuccessScore
Awarded 0–10 points per zip code across 10 demographic thresholds. Zip codes scoring ≥ 5 were labeled as viable restaurant locations (binary Success = 1).
↓
🌲
05 — TRAIN
Random Forest
Split data 80/20 for training and testing. One-hot encoded categorical features. Trained a Random Forest Regressor to predict SuccessScore from all zip-level features.

Key Insights

Why These Findings Matter

01
Wealth Index is the strongest predictor
A combination of median household income and median home value is the single best indicator of whether a zip code supports a restaurant. High-wealth neighborhoods mean more disposable income for dining out.
02
Population density beats raw population size
Large but spread-out populations don't drive foot traffic. Compact, dense zip codes consistently score higher — walkability matters more than headcount alone.
03
Net migration signals growth areas
Zip codes with positive net migration are gaining residents — meaning a growing customer base. These are ideal for early-mover advantage before competition moves in.
04
53% of LA zip codes are viable — but exact location still matters
Just over half of zip codes score 5+, but the top-scoring areas (7–8) cluster in specific corridors. Targeting these micro-markets dramatically improves survival odds.
05
Model accuracy is high enough to be actionable
With R² = 0.86 and MAE of only 0.22 score points, predictions are reliable enough to confidently rank zip codes — turning a guessing game into a data-driven strategy.
